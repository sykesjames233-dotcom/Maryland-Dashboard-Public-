Maryland Child Poverty & Economic Hardship Dashboard

ACS · SAIPE · BLS · MSDE · MD DHS · 2023–2024
About

This dashboard tracks child poverty and economic hardship across Maryland's 24 jurisdictions, integrating data on food assistance (SNAP, FARMS), income support (TCA/TANF), nutrition programs (WIC), unemployment, and per capita income. Child poverty — the share of residents under 18 below the federal poverty line — drives educational, health, and long-term economic outcomes. Click any county to zoom in and view school-level data.

📋 Data Sources & Methodology

CENSUS · SAIPE
Child & Overall Poverty Estimates
U.S. Census Bureau Small Area Income and Poverty Estimates (SAIPE). Model-based annual estimates of the share of residents under 18 and all ages living below the federal poverty level. Combines ACS survey data, IRS tax records, and SNAP administrative data. Overall poverty from ACS 5-year estimates (S1701), accessed via FRED.
census.gov/programs-surveys/saipe →
MD DHS · MDHS
SNAP Participation by County
SNAP average monthly participants per county from Maryland Hunger Solutions 2025 Hunger Profiles, sourced from Maryland Department of Human Services statistical reports (SFY 2024: July 2023–June 2024). Participation rates calculated against total county population. Statewide average: ~693,000 participants/month.
mdhungersolutions.org →
MSDE · FARMS
Free & Reduced Meal Rate (Schools)
Maryland State Department of Education Free and Reduced-Price Meals Data, School Year 2022–2023. Percentage of enrolled students qualifying for free or reduced-price school meals under the National School Lunch Program. Used as a school-level proxy for family economic hardship. School-level data also reflects Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) adoption.
marylandpublicschools.org →
BLS · LAUS
County Unemployment Rate
Bureau of Labor Statistics Local Area Unemployment Statistics (LAUS) program. Annual average unemployment rates by county, 2024. Rates are model-based estimates derived from Current Population Survey data, unemployment insurance records, and Census Bureau data. Maryland 2024 annual average: 3.0% (BLS USDL-25-0294).
bls.gov/lau →
BEA · FRED
Per Capita Personal Income
Bureau of Economic Analysis county-level per capita personal income, 2024 annual estimates. Accessed via St. Louis Federal Reserve FRED database. Includes wages, proprietors' income, dividends, interest, rent, and government transfer payments. TCA/TANF caseload estimates from Maryland DHS statistical reports (approximate values for reference).
fred.stlouisfed.org →
NOTE · CITATION
Methods, Definitions & Citation
Child poverty = persons under 18 in families below the OMB federal poverty threshold. Baltimore City is an independent city counted separately from Baltimore County. School locations are representative; individual school FARMS rates from MSDE SY 2022-23 data. County shapefile from Maryland iMAP (opendata.maryland.gov), reprojected WGS84. Dashboard built with Chart.js.
